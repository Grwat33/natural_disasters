What is a natural disaster?

Natural disasters are catastrophic events with atmospheric, geological, and hydrological origina that can cause fatalities, property damage and social environmental disruption.

How does FEMA declare a disaster?

Requests for disaster declaration are made by the Governor of the State affected by the disaster and then approved by the President of the United States. State also includes the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, Guam, American Samoa, and the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands. Also eligible to request a declaration and receive assistance are the Federated States of Micronesia and the Republic of Marshall Islands.
